Description of my work experience and also some personal interesting projects that I did over the years.

  • Development of a distribution system for the Brazilian startup GetNinjas

     The system interacts with a cloud environment and consumes thousands of requests per day finding the best match of professionals for a given service demand. It takes into consideration the geo-location of the professional and the service requester, as well as multiple variables of interest for business and service improvement. Concepts of optimization, clustering and probabilistic distributions are considered in the decision making. Got hooked on Python from here onwards.

  • IBM Research Brazil Internship

     2 years as a research intern at IBM. I developed tools in Java for Twitter crawling and sentiment analysis. Also collaborated with other researchers in areas such as agent based simulation applied to social networks. Was involved into multiple patent application processes.

  • Research and development for energy distribution systems at Daimon

     Participated in two projects for major energy distribution companies in Brazil.
    The first one aimed at long term planning for substation construction. I developed all the backend system and algorithms for demand forecast (applied dilation methods for image processing) and optimal substation construction (a mixed integer linear problem on the possible characteristics of each substation) and placement heuristics (particle swarm to find the best location in a mesh of 10s of thousands of possibilities).
    The second project was a heuristics for optimal placement of automatic switches in Sao Paulo. I developed a module based on a previously existing genetic algorithm tool that optimized the placement on a single branch of the power network, expanding it to run in the whole network. Also included automated pdf reporting. Both projects were done in C++ and the optimization tool was GLPK.

  • Multiple times as a TA

     I have been a TA since my undergrad. Taught Linear Programming, Numeric Calculus, Methods for Differential Equations in Calculus, Basic Math Proofs and Basic Python. Probably my most significant work here was to develop a GLPK based tool to solve simple linear problems and present them in them in the tableau form (as seen in Bazaraa et al.). The code was made in simple C and run as a batch program. I started working on a newer, better version in Visual C++, but I never made it available. Will dig these up when I have some time.
